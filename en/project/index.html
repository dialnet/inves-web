PROJECT

Collective Research Portal of Spanish Universities

WHAT IS INV.ES?

INV.es is the gateway to Spanish scientific production, a collaborative platform that unifies and makes visible the results of research carried out in public universities.

It is an initiative promoted by the Conference of Rectors of Spanish Universities (CRUE) and developed by the Dialnet Foundation, which aims to create a shared, sustainable information system governed by the academic community itself.

ORIGIN

Recovery, Transformation, and Resilience Plan

INV.ES emerges within the framework of the Recovery, Transformation, and Resilience Plan, through the UniDigital program, promoted by the Ministry of Universities to advance the modernization and digitalization of the Spanish university system.

The project is developed under Royal Decree 641/2021, which regulates the direct granting of subsidies to Spanish public universities for investment in digital infrastructures, technological developments, and collaborative inter-university projects of general interest to the university system.

In this context, the University of La Rioja, as coordinating university, presented in November 2021 the proposal for the inter-university project Collective Research Portal of Spanish Universities (INV.ES). The project was conceived from its inception as a collaborative initiative, aligned with the objectives of UniDigital and oriented towards generating a shared, reusable, and sustainable solution.

The technical management and development of the project have been undertaken by the Dialnet Foundation, which has made the Dialnet CRIS technology available to INV.ES, a consolidated infrastructure widely implemented in the university sector, guaranteeing interoperability, scalability, and utilization of the portal by the entire Spanish university system.

A COLLABORATIVE PROJECT

Spanish public universities and Dialnet Foundation

INV.ES is the result of collaboration between Spanish public universities, with the Dialnet Foundation as the entity responsible for technical management and technological development of the project.

In addition to the University of La Rioja, participants in the creation included the University of Santiago de Compostela, University of Burgos, University of Salamanca, University of Cádiz, University of La Laguna, Pablo de Olavide University, University of Vigo, University of León, University of Alcalá, University of A Coruña, University of Valladolid, and the National Distance Education University (UNED).

More public universities have joined these, reaching the current 29.

The solution is based on Dialnet CRIS technology, a consolidated and widely implemented system that allows integrating data from multiple institutions, ensuring their quality and offering them homogeneously, without dependencies on proprietary software. This approach ensures scalability, interoperability, and utilization of the portal by the entire university system.

BOOSTING OPEN SCIENCE

Transparency, accessibility and global collaboration

INV.ES is conceived from its origin as an infrastructure aligned with the principles of open science, understood as a model that promotes transparency, free access to scientific knowledge, and the reuse of research results to maximize their social, academic, and economic impact.

The portal facilitates unified access to the scientific production of Spanish public universities, prominently incorporating results available in open access and favoring their visibility and dissemination through a single consultation point. In this way, INV.ES contributes to reducing barriers to access to scientific information and to strengthening the role of universities as generators and transmitters of open knowledge.

Furthermore, INV.ES promotes interoperability and reuse of scientific information, through the standardization of data from multiple institutions and the adoption of common information models. This approach facilitates the exploitation of data by researchers, universities, institutional leaders, and other agents of the science, technology, and innovation system.

The portal also incorporates analysis and visualization tools that allow contextualizing research activity, identifying collaboration networks, and analyzing trends, contributing to greater transparency in the evaluation and monitoring of university research.

In line with international commitments on open science, INV.ES offers an aggregated presentation of scientific production linked to the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), highlighting the contribution of Spanish universities to major social, economic, and environmental challenges.

Through this approach, INV.ES establishes itself as a strategic infrastructure for advancing towards a more open, accessible, collaborative, and impact-oriented research ecosystem, strengthening knowledge transfer and the social return on public investment in research.

CHALLENGES

Strategic, technological, and organizational challenges

The development of INV.ES has involved addressing a series of strategic, technological, and organizational challenges, derived both from its inter-university character and from its vocation to serve the entire Spanish university system.

One of the main challenges is the integration of information from multiple universities, each with its own systems, organizational structures, and data models. To this end, INV.ES has worked on defining a shared common language, capable of harmonizing and standardizing scientific information, ensuring its coherence, quality, and comparability.

Associated with this, the project addresses the challenge of data deduplication and consolidation, especially regarding publications, authors, research groups, and institutional affiliations. The correct identification of researchers and results is key to offering reliable information and enabling advanced and reusable analyses.

Another relevant challenge is the development of a scalable and sustainable technological infrastructure, capable of growing progressively in number of institutions, data volume, and functionalities, without depending on proprietary solutions or limiting its adoption by new universities. INV.ES responds to this challenge by relying on consolidated technology such as Dialnet CRIS and a collaborative model of continuous evolution.

INV.ES also faces the challenge of balancing the informative function with the management function, offering a portal that not only serves as a showcase for university research, but can also be integrated into the internal processes of universities and support data-driven decision-making.

Likewise, the project faces the challenge of effectively promoting open science, incorporating open data sources, highlighting production in open access, and facilitating the reuse of information, while respecting the regulatory frameworks and institutional policies of each university.

Finally, INV.ES addresses the challenge of long-term governance and sustainability, establishing a shared model among participating universities that guarantees the evolution of the portal, its maintenance, and its adaptation to new needs of the university system and the research ecosystem.

IMPACT

INV.ES has a significant impact at different levels of the university and research system, contributing to improving the visibility, efficiency, and social value of research activities developed at Spanish public universities.

Visibility and Outreach

First, the project strengthens the visibility and outreach of university research by offering a single, aggregating access point to scientific production, researchers, research groups, and lines of work developed at different universities. This facilitates both national and international dissemination of knowledge and recognition of the role of universities in scientific and technological advancement.

Institutional Decision-Making

INV.ES also has a direct impact on institutional decision-making by providing indicators, analysis tools, and visualizations that allow universities and academic and management leaders to have reliable, updated, and comparable information. This approach favors more strategic, evidence-based research management aligned with institutional objectives and public policies.

Research Community

From the perspective of the research community, INV.ES contributes to improving access to scientific information, identifying collaboration opportunities, and analyzing research networks and trends, facilitating a more connected and collaborative environment between researchers and universities.

Promoting Open Science

The portal also has a relevant impact in promoting open science by highlighting production in open access, favoring data reuse, and increasing transparency of research activity funded with public funds. In this sense, INV.ES contributes to maximizing the social return on public investment in research.

Knowledge Transfer and Social Impact

INV.ES also strengthens knowledge transfer and social impact by making visible the contribution of university research to the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) and major social, economic, and environmental challenges. This perspective allows connecting scientific activity with society's needs and with national and international agendas.

Structural Impact

Finally, the project has a structural impact by consolidating a collaborative and sustainable model, based on shared, reusable, and scalable infrastructures, which strengthens inter-university cooperation and lays the foundations for the future evolution of the portal as a strategic node of the Spanish science, technology, and innovation system.
